Welcome to My OJT Diary

This is where I document my daily activities and learnings during my OJT.

About Me

Hello! Welcome to my journey as a trainee in the Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR). I am Hazib M. Tanandato, a third-year student at North Eastern Mindanao State University, studying Bachelor of Science in Computer Science and undertaking my On-the Job-Training (OJT)/Practicum with the required 162 hours.

Daily Logs

Day 1

On the first day of my On-the-Job Training (OJT), I attended the Monday Convocation Orientation at the Department of Environment and Natural Resources - Provincial Environment and Natural Resources Office (DENR-PENRO). The orientation gave me a comprehensive overview of the office’s operations and my role. Following this, I worked on editing all the OJT IDs for the office, ensuring that all information was accurate and up-to-date. It was a great start to my OJT, providing valuable insights and a chance to get acquainted with the office’s systems

Day 2

On the second day of my On-the-Job Training (OJT), we began with an orientation specifically about our OJT responsibilities and goals. The session was informative and set clear expectations for our tasks and learning objectives. After the orientation, I continued with the task of editing the OJT IDs, making sure that all the details were accurate and up-to-date. It was a productive day, as I was able to further familiarize myself with the office’s systems while progressing with my assigned tasks.

Day 3

On the third day of my On-the-Job Training (OJT), we focused on laminating the OJT IDs, ensuring they were durable and professional. After completing this task, I participated in general cleaning of the PICTU office, helping to organize and tidy up the workspace. It was a productive day, contributing both to administrative tasks and maintaining a clean work environment.

Day 4

On the fourth day of my On-the-Job Training (OJT), our task involved collecting files from the Planning Office and transferring them to another office. This task required careful handling and organization of documents to ensure they were properly relocated. It was a day of hands-on work, contributing to the smooth management and organization of office resources.

Day 5

On the fifth day of my On-the-Job Training (OJT), I was assigned to the Monitoring & Enforcement Office for preventive maintenance tasks. This involved performing routine checks and maintenance to ensure the office equipment and systems were in good working order. It was a valuable experience in understanding the importance of regular upkeep and ensuring everything functions efficiently.

Day 6

On the sixth day of my On-the-Job Training (OJT), my colleagues and I worked on UTP cable crimping and testing. We carefully crimped the cables to ensure proper connections and tested them to verify their functionality. This hands-on task provided practical experience in network setup and maintenance, enhancing our technical skills.

Day 7

On the seventh day of my On-the-Job Training (OJT), I was assigned to enhance the background of the Wi-Fi portal at DENR-PENRO. This task involved updating the visual design to improve the portal’s appearance and user experience. It was a great opportunity to apply my design skills and contribute to a more polished and professional look for the office’s Wi-Fi access point.

Day 8

On the eighth day of my On-the-Job Training (OJT), my partner and I were assigned to the Payroll Office to input important data. We meticulously entered various details to ensure accuracy and completeness in the payroll system. This task was crucial for maintaining up-to-date and precise payroll records, providing us with hands-on experience in data management.

Day 9

On the ninth day of my On-the-Job Training (OJT), I was assigned to the next office within the Monitoring & Enforcement Office to perform preventive maintenance. This involved conducting routine checks and ensuring that all equipment and systems were functioning properly. It was an opportunity to apply the preventive maintenance skills I had previously learned and contribute to the overall efficiency of the office.

Day 10

On the tenth day of my On-the-Job Training (OJT), my colleagues and I worked on printer networking. We set up and configured the printers to ensure they were properly connected to the network, allowing for seamless printing from multiple workstations. This task enhanced our understanding of network management and provided practical experience in setting up office equipment.

Day 11

On the eleventh day of my On-the-Job Training (OJT), my colleagues and I performed CCTV maintenance. We inspected and serviced the cameras, ensuring they were functioning correctly and providing clear surveillance. This task gave us valuable hands-on experience in maintaining security systems, highlighting the importance of regular checks to ensure safety and reliability.

Day 12

On the twelfth day of my On-the-Job Training (OJT), I assembled a system unit in the PICTU office. This task involved putting together the various components of a computer, ensuring that everything was correctly connected and functioning. It was a hands-on experience that deepened my understanding of computer hardware and assembly.

Day 13

On the thirteenth day of my On-the-Job Training (OJT), my partner and I disassembled and reassembled a system unit, ensuring that all components were correctly handled and reconnected. Afterward, we worked on SD card partitioning, organizing the storage to meet specific requirements. This day was filled with technical tasks that enhanced our skills in hardware management and data organization.

Day 14

On the fourteenth day of my On-the-Job Training (OJT), I focused on booting and resetting a PC. This task involved troubleshooting the system to ensure it started up correctly and performing a reset to restore it to optimal working condition. It was a practical exercise in managing system issues and maintaining computer performance.

Day 15

On the fifteenth day of my On-the-Job Training (OJT), we celebrated the International Day for the Conservation of the Mangrove Ecosystem by engaging in some field activities. These actions were focused on the preservation and protection of mangroves, allowing us to contribute directly to environmental conservation efforts. It was a meaningful day that highlighted the importance of protecting our natural ecosystems.

Day 16

On the sixteenth day of my On-the-Job Training (OJT), my supervisor assigned me and a colleague to perform preventive maintenance. I was given the responsibility to guide my colleague through the process, ensuring that we followed proper procedures and checked all necessary equipment. It was a great opportunity to apply my knowledge and take on a leadership role in maintaining the office's systems.

Day 17

On the seventeenth day of my On-the-Job Training (OJT), we attended an earthquake drill orientation conducted at DENR-PENRO. The session was important for understanding safety protocols and preparedness measures in case of an earthquake. After the orientation, we returned to our regular OJT tasks, continuing with our usual activities and responsibilities.

Day 18

On the eighteenth day of my On-the-Job Training (OJT), we conducted an orientation on earthquake drills. This involved presenting and explaining the procedures and safety measures to our colleagues, ensuring that everyone was well-informed and prepared for emergency situations. It was a valuable experience in sharing knowledge and contributing to workplace safety.

Day 19

On the nineteenth day of my On-the-Job Training (OJT), we worked on connecting a router. We connected the router to PCs, with our supervisor guiding us through the process. This task involved setting up the network to ensure proper connectivity and functionality. It was a practical experience in network configuration and troubleshooting..

Day 20

On the twentieth and final day of my On-the-Job Training (OJT), I and my colleague was assigned to edit the certificates for our team. This task involved updating and finalizing the certificates to ensure they were accurate and professionally presented. It was a fitting conclusion to my OJT, as it allowed me to contribute to a meaningful aspect of our team’s achievements.
